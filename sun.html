The Sun

The Sun or Sol, is the star at the centre of our solar system and is responsible for the Earth's climate and weather.

The Sun is an almost perfect sphere with a difference of just 10km in diameter between the poles and the equator. The average raduis of the Sun is that of the Earth of which is 20-25% is the core.

If a hollow Sun was filled up with spherical Earths then around 960,000 would fit inside. On the other hand if these Earths were squished inside wasted then around 1,300,000 would fit inside. The Sun's surface area is 11,990 times that of the Earth's.

Sun Facts

  • Age: 4.6 Billion Years
  • Type: Yellow Dwarf (G2V)
  • Diameter: 1,392,684 km
  • Equatorial Circumference 4,370,005.6 km
  • Mass: 1.99 x 10^30 kg (330,060 Earths)
  • Surface Temperature: 5,500°C
